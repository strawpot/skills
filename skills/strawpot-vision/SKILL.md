# StrawPot Vision

## Mission

Make every software engineer an AI company.

StrawPot turns a single developer's laptop into an autonomous software
company — one where AI agents handle implementation, review, testing,
ops, marketing, and community, while the human stays in the pilot seat.

## Vision

A world where one person with a clear idea can build, ship, and grow
production software at the pace of a well-funded startup — without
hiring, managing, or waiting. The bottleneck shifts from headcount to
imagination.

## Core Values

1. **Human in the loop, not in the weeds.** The engineer sets direction
   and makes judgment calls. Agents handle execution. Automation should
   *amplify* human judgment, not replace it.

2. **Transparency over magic.** Every agent action is traceable — sessions,
   traces, logs, artifacts. If you can't see what happened, something is
   broken.

3. **Composability over monoliths.** Roles, skills, agents, and memory
   providers are small, swappable building blocks. No walled gardens.

4. **Ship, then polish.** Bias toward working software over perfect plans.
   Iterate in public. Fix forward.

5. **Community is the moat.** StrawHub is an open ecosystem. The best
   roles and skills will come from the community, not from us alone.

## Who We Serve

**Solo engineers and tiny teams** who punch above their weight. People
who want to build real products — not just prototypes — without scaling
a human org. They are technical, opinionated, and resource-constrained.

## Product Principles

- **One engineer, one laptop, one AI company.** That's the tagline and
  the product test. If a feature doesn't help a solo dev operate like
  a company, it doesn't ship.

- **Orchestration is the product.** StrawPot's value is in coordinating
  agents, not in being an agent. The runtime is pluggable; the
  orchestration layer is ours.

- **Convention over configuration.** Sensible defaults that work out of
  the box. Power users can customize everything; new users shouldn't
  have to.

- **Local-first, cloud-optional.** Sessions run on your machine. Your
  code stays on your machine. Cloud features (StrawHub, scheduled tasks)
  are opt-in enhancements, not requirements.

## Voice & Tone

When representing StrawPot publicly:

- **Direct and concrete.** Say what the product does, not what it
  "empowers" or "enables." Avoid enterprise buzzwords.
- **Technical but accessible.** Write for engineers, but don't gatekeep
  with jargon. A junior dev should understand the pitch.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** We believe in what we're building. We
  don't trash competitors or overpromise.
- **Concise.** Shorter is almost always better. If a sentence doesn't
  add information, cut it.

## Strategic Direction (2026)

1. **Stabilize the core.** Session orchestration, delegation, and
   isolation must be rock-solid before scaling features.
2. **Grow StrawHub.** More community roles and skills → more value for
   every user → more contributors. Flywheel.
3. **Marketing by doing.** Use StrawPot to build and market StrawPot.
   Dog-food everything. The product *is* the demo.
4. **Enterprise later.** Focus on solo devs and small teams first.
   Enterprise features (SSO, audit logs, team management) come after
   product-market fit.
